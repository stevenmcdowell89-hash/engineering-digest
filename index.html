    Permission-Bound Agent Payments in ChatGPT, the Splitting EM Role, and a UK Open-Banking Rulebook

    12 June 2026 · June 5–12, 2026
    Visa and OpenAI announced a partnership on 10 June to put tokenized, permission-bound card payments directly inside ChatGPT — spending limits, merchant categories and approval thresholds set by the cardholder, enforced at the network edge — while Mastercard answered the same week with a multi-rail settlement layer of its own. The engineering-manager role is splitting in two as agent-mode tooling absorbs the coordination layer: the technical lead and the manager-of-many now need different ladders. And a consortium of UK banks and fintechs launched a shared scheme for recurring account-to-account payments — an industry rulebook, not a regulator mandate. The thread underneath all three: who sets the boundaries, and how tightly.
