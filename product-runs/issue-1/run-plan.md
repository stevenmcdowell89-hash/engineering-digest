# Run Plan — Product Edition Issue 1 (June 2026)

Window: 13 May – 12 June 2026. State read: no prior issues, `trend_history` empty — no recurrence checks apply. 23 candidates in `candidates.json`.

## Through-thread

Spend is moving onto rails and surfaces where card-linked loyalty cannot yet follow — agent checkouts, account-to-account schemes, the OS wallet — while UK banks recondition what rewards mean on the surfaces they still control.

## Trends (4, ordered by significance)

### Trend 01 — Agentic commerce builds its trust layer (and leaves loyalty out)
The month's biggest single delta: a same-day (10 June) network double-header. Visa announced Agent Score, an Agentic Directory and a Large Transaction Model at its Payments Forum, plus a partnership embedding Visa payments across OpenAI's agentic commerce; Mastercard launched Agent Pay for Machines with 30+ partners and multi-rail settlement. None of the three announcements mentions offers or rewards.
- Feeding pieces (all in-window): Visa Payments Forum (10 Jun, Business Wire); Visa–OpenAI (10 Jun, Visa Newsroom); Mastercard AP4M (10 Jun, Mastercard Newsroom).
- Closer: **investigate** ("What it looks like in product →") — evidenced gap: the verification/readiness layer that will decide what agents can see and transact with is being specified now, and the three announcements describe credentials, directories and merchant-readiness scoring with no loyalty or offer layer in any of them. Sourced to the three releases themselves.
- AI/agentic is bounded to this single section per the breadth rule.

### Trend 02 — Pay by Bank goes operational: the UK gets its first new payment scheme since 2008
UKPI's commercial VRP scheme went live 2 June (31 firms incl. all major UK retail banks; Wave 1 regulated sectors, Wave 2 e-commerce H2 2026). GoCardless launched Recurring Pay by Bank on it the same day, explicitly positioned against the card networks' UK fee take; TrueLayer launched Bank on File (card-on-file equivalent for bank payments) with named live merchants.
- Feeding pieces (all in-window): FCA statement on UKPI (2 Jun); GoCardless launch (2 Jun); TrueLayer Bank on File (3 Jun, IT Brief UK — exact day to verify in Phase 4).
- Closer: **investigate** — evidenced gap (the spec's own A2A example, now operational): a card-linked offer needs a card transaction to read; recurring spend settling over UKPI's A2A rails produces no card transaction. Both legs sourced.
- Statline visual earned: 2008 (last new UK scheme) / 31 firms / H2 2026 (e-commerce wave).

### Trend 03 — The OS wallet bids to be where loyalty lives
Apple's WWDC week (8–12 Jun): iOS 27 Wallet scans any physical loyalty card into Apple Wallet and Tap to Share passes loyalty identity to merchants in-store — the OS-level container competing with the banking app as the offer surface. Supporting: Visa Flexible Credential went live in the UK on Zilch cards on 12 May (one day before the window opens — original date stated in the section), putting multi-option payment + rewards logic inside a single credential.
- Feeding pieces: iOS 27 Wallet (10 Jun, MacRumors + Apple Newsroom); Visa Flexible Credential UK (12 May, Visa Newsroom UK, near-window flagged).
- Closer: **why** (context) — surface competition is real but no roadmap-grade instantiation or gap is sourced this month.

### Trend 04 — UK banks recondition rewards around behaviour
Chase UK doubled cashback to 2% but made it behaviour-conditional (15 transactions/month + £1,000 in savings; criteria live 1 June). Nationwide confirmed another £100 direct member payout (21 May results) — cash distribution rather than merchant-funded offers. Tesco widened Clubcard redemption into travel insurance at 1:1 (late May — exact date imprecise; Phase 4 to verify the URL resolves and date the piece honestly as "May").
- Feeding pieces: Chase UK (1 Jun, MoneySavingExpert); Nationwide (21 May, Be Clever With Your Cash — figures to double-check against results statement); Tesco Clubcard (May, PA syndication).
- Closer: **why** (context) — three different answers to "what is loyalty value": engagement-gated cashback, direct cash, redemption breadth.

## Roadmap strip (2 threads, both evidenced gaps — no inventions, no instructions)

1. **The A2A coverage gap** (→ Trend 02). Card-linked offers read card transactions; UKPI moves recurring spend to rails that produce none. Sources: FCA UKPI statement; GoCardless launch post.
2. **Loyalty absent from the agent rails** (→ Trend 01). The agentic trust layer (Agent Score, Agentic Directory, AP4M credentials) is being specified without an offers/rewards object in it. Sources: the three 10 June releases.
